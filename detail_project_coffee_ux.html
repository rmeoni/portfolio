Coffee Shop UX Research/Design
Client
Café Don Lucas
Release
05/19/2024


In this project, I followed the Design Thinking approach. I began with qualitative and quantitative research to define user personas and identify pain points. Next, I used a user journey map to ideate solutions and created wireframes and prototypes in Figma. Finally, I conducted usability testing to refine the design based on user feedback. Once the design was finalized, I developed the site using React.

Case Study

Project Overview

The Problem

The company faced significant challenges managing orders from their business-to-business (B2B) customers without an online store. Additionally, they were uncertain about the viability of transitioning to a subscription-based model for retail customers.

The Goal

To conduct in-depth research to understand the coffee market landscape in the country, along with the specific needs of end users. The objective was to design a seamless online ordering system that would be accessible to a wide range of users while also gathering the insights needed to evaluate the feasibility of a subscription model.

My Role

As a User Experience Designer and Developer, I led the project’s research and design process by conducting both quantitative and qualitative studies. My responsibilities included prototyping solutions, running usability tests, and ensuring the final design provided an intuitive and efficient user experience. I also developed the app in React, ensuring every feature functioned seamlessly.

Responsibilities

I was tasked with researching user behaviors and business needs, synthesizing that data into actionable insights, and translating them into a functional, user-friendly solution. This solution not only streamlined the company’s order-taking process but also provided the necessary data to assess whether a subscription model was viable for their retail customers.


User Research

Summary

As part of our quantitative research into coffee consumption in Guatemala, we analyzed national trends and behaviors. The country is renowned for its strong coffee culture, with many individuals consuming an average of 2-4 cups per day, aligning with global consumption patterns. To deepen our understanding, I conducted interviews with over 50 participants aged 25-45, targeting those with the appropriate demographics and interests. This research revealed key insights: participants not only enjoy their daily coffee but also prioritize convenience in their purchasing habits. A significant number expressed a strong preference for online shopping to avoid traffic and save time. Additionally, the majority showed interest in subscribing to a coffee service, motivated by potential cost savings. For a broader context on coffee consumption in Guatemala, sources such as the International Coffee Organization (ICO) and the Guatemala Coffee Association (Anacafé) provide valuable data on national consumption habits and trends. This research enabled us to grasp both the quantitative and qualitative aspects of our users' needs, guiding our decision-making in developing a user-friendly coffee online store.

Post-launch Usability Study Plan

Introduction

  • Title: Creating an ordering web app for Café Don Lucas
  • Author: UX Rodrigo, UX Designer, hello@uxrodrigo.com
  • Stakeholders: Café Don Lucas Owners
  • Date: 11/18/2024
  • Project background: We are creating a Café Don Lucas web app to take orders online for their wholesale customers as well as to attract new ones. We want to create a product that can compete in the market, improve sales, and increase customer satisfaction.
  • Research goals: We’d like to figure out what specific difficulties users encounter when they try to complete the core tasks of the Café Don Lucas’ app: item selection, ordering, and in-app navigation.

Research Questions

  • Are users able to successfully order the coffee that they want?
  • How long does it take for a user to select and order coffee in the app?
  • What can we learn from the steps users took to order coffee?
  • Are there any parts of the coffee-ordering process at which users are getting stuck?

KPIs

  • Time on task: How much time users spend ordering coffee.
  • Conversion rates: How much coffee customers are ordering.
  • User error rates: How often users get stuck trying to order the coffee they want.

Methodology

  • Type: Unmoderated usability study.
  • Location: Guatemala, remote (participants will go through the usability study in their own homes).
  • Date: Sessions will take place between November 21 and 22.
  • 5 participants will order coffee through the app. Each participant will then complete a questionnaire on their experience.
  • Each session will last for 10–20 minutes.

Participants

  • Participants can be anyone who orders coffee online.
  • Participants should be between 18 and 62 years old.
  • Participants should include a fairly even distribution of genders across the spectrum and people with different abilities, including 1 user who isn’t fluent in Spanish.
  • Incentive: 1 free bag of coffee.

Accesibility

To prioritize accessibility, we developed the website to cater to a diverse customer base by offering both Spanish and English language options. This ensures that all users, regardless of their language preference, can seamlessly navigate and interact with the site. Additionally, we incorporated Dark and Light mode options to accommodate different visual preferences, improving usability in various lighting conditions. These features work together to enhance the overall user experience, making the platform more inclusive and adaptable to users’ needs.

Takeaways

  1. User Preferences: The research highlighted that customers value quality over price when it comes to coffee consumption. Many participants indicated a willingness to pay a fair price for premium coffee rather than opting for cheaper products.
  2. Subscription Model Feasibility: While there was interest in a subscription model, the company ultimately decided against it. They concluded that entering the subscription market might compromise their brand’s focus on quality and could lead to a perception of offering lower-tier products.
  3. Enhanced Customer Experience: Rather than shifting to a subscription-based retail model, the company chose to invest in improving the overall customer experience. This includes streamlining the order process for their existing business-to-business customers and enhancing service delivery.
  4. Focus on Fair Pricing: The decision to maintain a fair and profitable pricing strategy allows the company to uphold its commitment to quality. This approach ensures that they can continue to provide premium products while fostering loyalty among their current customers.
  5. Long-Term Customer Relationships: By prioritizing customer experience and quality over discount strategies, the company aims to build stronger, long-term relationships with its B2B clients, ensuring sustainable growth and profitability in the competitive coffee market.
